FAQ

Where the heck do I get storage boxes?

Harvard is providing some free boxes to students, and you can find out where you can pick up boxes on the Harvard Coronavirus FAQ. Search (ctrl-f) for “pick up boxes”.

What payment options are there?

  • Venmo: no fee
  • Cash: no fee; exact change required
  • Credit Card or Debit Card: ~3% fee from PayPal >:(

How can I confirm that there is enough space to store my boxes?

After you submit the Google Form, Bob will send you an email within 12 hours to confirm that he has space for your boxes.

What if I find other storage after I submit the Google Form?

That's fine. You don't have to pay Bob until you drop off your boxes. You're chillin.

You can also try the HSA store, Staples, or Target.

What if I'm leaving campus before Saturday? Can I drop off my boxes before Saturday?

Please submit the Google Form and directly contact Bob at storagewithbob@gmail.com about your situation.

What if I want to access my stuff before to September?

That complicates things. We cannot guarantee that we will be around Cambridge area from April to August. If you want, please directly contact Bob at storagewithbob@gmail.com to see if we can figure something out.

What if I need to store my stuff after September?

That shouldn't be a big issue. If we have already rented the storage garage up to September, then we can easily renew our rental for additional months. If you plan to do this, please directly contact Bob at storagewithbob@gmail.com

My boxes are the HEAVY and my arms are the WEAK. Help!

You can borrow a hand dolly from YardOps or your Building Manager's office, if you leave your Student ID or Credit Card as collateral. It makes moving boxes a lot easier.

What can I store?
  1. No open foods or drugs.
    • I asked the manager at ExtraSpace Storage what is allowed and he said “absolutely no open foods or drugs… like prescription drugs are fine but not illegal drugs”. We will not search your stuff. However, if rats or pests find your food or illegal drugs in the storage facility, the storage manager will throw your boxes out and we are not liable for your stuff.
  2. We will only store boxes or box-shaped items (for example, mini-fridges and microwaves).
    • If your stuff is not in a box, but it is box-shaped (for example, a mini-fridge), then please email Bob at storagewithbob@gmail.com and he will check beforehand if your item will fit in alongside our other boxes in the storage garage.
    • In most cases, it’s not a problem and you will be fine. However, don’t try to screw us over with some wack shit. If you are in doubt, please put it into a box or email Bob.
  3. Anything else is fine, as long as it is stored inside a box.
    • Remember, Harvard is providing boxes, and you can find out where you can pick up boxes on the Harvard Coronavirus FAQ. Search (ctrl-f) for “pick up boxes”.
  4. You should know that the storage facility is air-conditioned at about 70 degrees Fahrenheit, or 21 degrees Celsius. Keep this in mind when you decide what to store and what to take home.
